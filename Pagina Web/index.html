Capítulo 1

A Sea of Hope

A cloud named Nimbita, while traveling the world, discovered something surprising on a remote island called American Samoa: a vital secret was about to be revealed.

The ocean, a silent but powerful warrior, absorbed around 31% of the carbon dioxide (CO₂) that humans generated daily through their activities.

It acted as a protective shield against climate change. However, while the waves whispered ancient stories of balance, the threat of acidification to emerge as a new and fearsome adversary.

Capítulo 2

The Call of the Reef

The sea, a brave warrior, felt that over the years its task was becoming increasingly difficult. One day, it observed a group of clouds starting to cover the sky and realized that they were no longer as white as they used to be.

Worried, it asked for help, as its only allies, the corals, showed alarming signs of environmental stress: they were starting to bleach, a clear sign that something was wrong.

The acidification, caused by excess carbon dioxide (CO₂), which the sea absorbed at around 40% of global CO₂ emissions in recent years (between 2015 and 2018), caused continuous acidification with a pH decrease of 0.0016 units per year.

This equates to an increase in acidity from 0.4% to 0.5% annually, contributing to the acidification of ocean waters and affecting entire ecosystems, including coral reefs that host 25% of all marine species, and reducing fish populations

The sea, exhausted by this constant battle, saw its strength wane.

Among the clouds in the sky was one named Nimbita, an ally of the sea, who decided to intervene

As she moved toward the island of Samoa, she noticed a group of children playing on the beach.

Approaching the reef, the children observed with sadness that the corals had lost their color and were much fewer than they remembered.

Perplexed, they turned to the approaching cloud and asked what was happening.

Capítulo 3

The Storm of Knowledge

Nimbita, with a soft yet worried voice, explained that this was the result of a battle her friend, the sea, was losing.

"The sea has been fighting pollution for years," she told them. "CO₂, along with other gases like methane (CH₄), is causing water acidification, and the corals cannot withstand it. They are dying slowly."

She spoke of the effort she made to clean the air throughout her travels around the world, but how, increasingly, the task was becoming more difficult.

"The air is not as pure as it used to be," she said. "And when the air is polluted, the sea also suffers." As Nimbita recounted the story

her voice became more solemn, almost like an echo of the sea itself. She told them about the endless battle the sea was waging against CO₂, a battle of years that had left great losses in its wake.

The children, moved, understood that protecting the air and the sea was vital to preserving not just the corals, but everything they loved about nature.

Nimbita mentioned that, during storms, they intensified, sea levels rose, and marine ecosystems were in danger

Nimbita, together with the children, decided it was time to act. On their journey, they visited the Observatory in American Samoa, where scientists monitored the climate and the sea's health.

There, they learned about the importance of research and how the collected data could help make informed decisions to protect the planet.

The children marveled at how scientists used advanced technology to study the sea. They mentioned that the sea acts as a carbon sink and explained that since the start of the industrial era.

the global ocean's average pH has decreased by 0.1 units, representing an increase of approximately 26% in acidity. This process of acidification adversely affects corals, mollusks, and other calcifying organisms, weakening their structures and making growth difficult.

They understood that knowledge was a powerful tool in the fight against climate change. They decided they should share what they learned with their community.

With Nimbita’s help, they organized workshops and activities to educate others about the importance of taking care of the air and the ocean.

Capítulo 4

The Power of Community

As the children shared their message, the community began to unite.

They planted trees in local parks, organized beach cleanups, and promoted the use of clean energy. Nimbita's story became a symbol of hope and action.

The clouds, once seemingly forgotten, were now seen as allies in the fight for cleaner air.

Citizens began to adopt more sustainable habits, reducing the use of plastics and choosing public transport or bicycles over cars

Local initiatives flourished, and soon, the small town became an example of how collective action can make a difference.

The children, alongside Nimbita, felt proud to be part of a movement bigger than themselves.

Join the fight for a cleaner and healthier planet. Every action counts, from planting a tree to reducing your carbon footprint.

Be part of the change; protect the ocean and the atmosphere for a future full of life and hope.
